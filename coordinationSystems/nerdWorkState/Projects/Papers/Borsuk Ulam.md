## Equivalent results[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Borsuk%E2%80%93Ulam_theorem&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Equivalent results")]

Above we showed how to prove the Borsukâ€“Ulam theorem from Tucker's lemma. The converse is also true: it is possible to prove Tucker's lemma from the Borsukâ€“Ulam theorem. Therefore, these two theorems are equivalent. There are several fixed-point theorems which come in three equivalent variants: anÂ [algebraic topology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algebraic_topology "Algebraic topology")Â variant, a combinatorial variant and a set-covering variant.
